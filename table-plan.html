Public Information Table — Plan

Coordinator guide for RVARC Field Day 2026

Download the printable version of this document: PDF Word (.docx)

Purpose

The Public Information Table is RVARC's welcome point for visitors to Field Day. It makes amateur radio accessible and inviting to anyone who stops by — from curious neighbors to parents with children to local officials. A staffed, well-stocked table qualifies for a 100-point bonus under ARRL Field Day rules (Section 7.3.4).

Table Location

Position the table at or near the main entrance to the Field Day site — visible from the parking area and easy for first-time visitors to find without wandering through the operating area.

Staffing

  • Designate a Table Coordinator responsible for setup, supplies, and the visitor sign-up sheet
  • Recruit 2–3 club members willing to take 2-hour shifts as greeters
  • Greeters do not need to be technical experts — a friendly, welcoming manner matters most
  • Have one or two members available to give informal site tours for interested visitors

Materials Checklist

ARRL Handouts (print from arrl.org/brochures-and-exhibit-materials)

These are free PDFs. Print with RVARC contact info added to the blank side before printing.
  • "What is Amateur Radio?" flyer
  • ARRL Membership flyer
  • Youth & STEM flyer
  • "Serving Your Community" flyer
  • "What is Field Day" backgrounder (from ARRL Field Day PR Kit)

RVARC-Produced Documents

  • RVARC Club One-Pager (history, meetings, website, how to join)
  • "How to Get Licensed" info sheet (study resources, VE exam dates)
  • Visitor Sign-Up Sheet (name / email / phone — for post-event follow-up)

Table Setup

  • Table covering or tablecloth
  • ARRL or RVARC banner or sign (if available)
  • Brochure/document stand or accordion organizer
  • Pens (for sign-up sheet)

Greeter Talking Points

When visitors approach, a simple, friendly opening goes a long way:

"Welcome! We're the Rappahannock Valley Amateur Radio Club. Have you heard of ham radio? We'd love to tell you about it — and if you're interested, you can actually try making a contact yourself right over there."

Key points to hit:

  • Ham radio works when cell towers and internet fail — it's a critical emergency backup
  • RVARC members support Stafford, Fredericksburg, and Spotsylvania emergency communications
  • Anyone can get a license — the Technician exam has 35 questions, no Morse code, and most people pass with 4–8 hours of study
  • The GOTA station nearby lets visitors try making a contact with no license required
  • Point them to the sign-up sheet if they want to stay in touch

Post-Event Follow-Up

  • Collect the sign-up sheet at the end of the event
  • Pass it to the club secretary or membership chair within one week
  • Send a brief welcome email to everyone on the list — mention upcoming meetings and VE exam sessions
  • Note any elected officials or media contacts who visited for the Field Day report
